# Smoking vs. Cancer Risk Spreadsheet

Spreadsheet comparing approximate **lifetime lung cancer risk** for:

1. People who smoke  
2. People who do not smoke  
3. People who do not smoke but are exposed to secondhand smoke  

## Files

| File | Description |
|------|-------------|
| [`smoking_vs_cancer_risk.xlsx`](smoking_vs_cancer_risk.xlsx) | Full workbook (Summary, Detailed Data, Sources, Chart Data) |
| [`smoking_vs_cancer_risk_summary.csv`](smoking_vs_cancer_risk_summary.csv) | One-sheet summary for quick viewing |

## Quick numbers (rounded)

| Group | Approx. lifetime lung cancer risk |
|-------|-----------------------------------|
| Smokers | ~15% (~150 per 1,000) |
| Never-smokers (no secondhand smoke) | ~1.5% (~15 per 1,000) |
| Never-smokers with secondhand smoke | ~1.9% (~19 per 1,000) |

Secondhand smoke raises never-smokers’ lung cancer risk by about **20–30%** relative to unexposed never-smokers (U.S. Surgeon General). Absolute risk stays much closer to never-smokers than to smokers.

Figures are population estimates from CDC, NCI, ACS, Surgeon General, and peer-reviewed studies—not personal medical advice. See the **Sources** sheet in the Excel file for citations.
